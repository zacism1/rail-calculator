The required lengthening of the rail, to calibrate the rail to the DSFT (37 °C), can be determined with: e = L0 × 0.1115 × (37 - TR) e = Desired rail extension in millimetres (mm) L0 = Initial length of unstressed rail in meters (m) TR = Current average rail temperature in degrees Celsius (°C) The force necessary to achieve the desired rail extension can be determined using: F = MR × 0.031111 × (37 - TR) F = Force required to pull the rail, measured in ton-force MR = Weight of the rail per meter in kilograms (kg) TR = Current average rail temperature in degrees Celsius (°C) This pulling force calculation should be based on the actual average temperature of the rail that is to be de-stressed. Roller spacing: tangent/R≥800 every 12 sleepers, 800>R≥500 every 5, 500>R≥400 every 3, R<400 every 2. OTT is minimum 40 meters outside of stressing length ITT is the first sleeper inside the stressing length
